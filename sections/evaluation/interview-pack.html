Evaluation · Chapter 17

Interview pack

The evaluation interview pack prepares you to discuss AI quality assurance design credibly: golden dataset construction, CI integration, and continuous evaluation for drift, backed by a concrete story rather than a general statement that evaluation matters.

Calculating reading timeHands-on labQuiz

In plain terms

It is like preparing to explain a product's actual quality assurance pipeline, not just that quality matters to you.

How it works

In practice it comes down to STAR-style stories about a real evaluation system you built or debugged, paired with technical answers distinguishing golden datasets, CI-gated regression checks, and continuous production evaluation for drift.

A concrete example

A strong answer to how would you make sure this AI feature doesn't regress describes a specific golden dataset, a CI gate that blocks a regression before merge, and a continuous evaluation cadence that catches drift after launch.

Trade-offs

  • Breadth vs depth: Cover enough examples to show range, but go deep enough that interviewers can see your judgment.
  • Humility vs ownership: A strong answer acknowledges team context while clearly naming your own decisions.
  • Polish vs authenticity: Prepared stories help, but over-scripted answers sound evasive under follow-up questions.

Try it in code

const answer = {
  situation: "A prompt change regressed structured output formatting",
  cause: "no CI-gated eval check existed for that prompt",
  fix: "added a golden case + schema check to the CI pipeline",
  result: "the same class of regression is now caught pre-merge"
};

Change the inputs and watch which assumption breaks first. The gap between what you expected and what you got is where the learning is.

Hands-on lab

Task: Prepare one story about a quality regression that reached or almost reached production, what evaluation gap allowed it, and the specific fix you made to the evaluation process.

  1. Draft the answer with situation, task, action, and result kept separate.
  2. Add two measurable outcomes: latency reduced, cost saved, incidents avoided, revenue protected, or team impact.
  3. Write one likely follow-up question and answer it without changing the underlying facts.

Done when: you can deliver the answer in under two minutes and the follow-up answer doesn't contradict the original story.

Common mistakes

  • Running an evaluation once before launch is not the same as having a real evaluation strategy; interviewers probe specifically for CI integration and continuous evaluation, since that's what catches regressions and drift after the initial launch.

Which of these is true about strong interview answers?

Going deeper

Questions interviewers ask

  1. Explain Interview pack in two minutes to a senior engineer who has not shipped AI systems.
  2. You must pick a side of "Breadth vs depth" for a real product. Defend your choice, and name the signal that would make you reverse it.
  3. What breaks first in production, and how would you detect it before users do?
