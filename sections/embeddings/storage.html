Embeddings · Chapter 12

Storage

Storage for embeddings covers how vectors are persisted and organized, including choice of vector database or index, whether to compress vectors with quantization, and how to keep vectors consistent with their source content as it's updated or deleted. This chapter turns that idea into a traceable learning slice with examples, checks, and failure modes.

Common misunderstandings

  • Vector storage is not set-and-forget once vectors are inserted; when source content is updated or deleted, the corresponding vectors must be updated or removed too, or retrieval will return stale or orphaned results that no longer match reality.
  • Storage is not complete when the happy path works once; it needs representative evals and visible failure handling.
  • Do not hide Storage inside prompt text when it requires code, policy, data, or operational ownership.

Trade-offs

  • Quality vs latency: The main trade-off is storing full-precision vectors preserves maximum retrieval accuracy, but quantized or compressed vectors cost significantly less to store and search at large scale, with a measurable but often acceptable accuracy trade-off
  • Flexibility vs reliability: Open-ended designs adapt to more inputs; constrained contracts are easier to test and operate.
  • Automation vs oversight: More automation reduces manual work but needs stronger logging, review points, and rollback paths.
