Distributed training

Distributed training splits a model's training workload across multiple GPUs or machines, either by dividing the data, data parallelism, or dividing the model itself, model parallelism, to train faster or handle models too large for a single device. This chapter turns that idea into a traceable learning slice with examples, checks, and failure modes.

Beginner explanation

If you have no AI background, start here: Distributed training splits a model's training workload across multiple GPUs or machines, either by dividing the data, data parallelism, or dividing the model itself, model parallelism, to train faster or handle models too large for a single device.

Analogy: It is like a large printing job split across multiple printers running simultaneously, versus one printer handling the entire job alone, either splitting the pages, data parallelism, or splitting a single oversized document across machines, model parallelism.

Common misunderstandings

  • Adding more GPUs to a training run does not always produce a proportional speedup; communication overhead between devices can eat into the gains, especially if the workload isn't large enough to justify the coordination cost.
  • Distributed training is not complete when the happy path works once; it needs representative evals and visible failure handling.
  • Do not hide Distributed training inside prompt text when it requires code, policy, data, or operational ownership.

Trade-offs

  • Quality vs latency: The main trade-off is distributing training speeds up large training runs and enables training models too big for one device, but coordinating multiple devices adds real communication overhead and engineering complexity that isn't worth it for models that train fast enough on a single device
  • Flexibility vs reliability: Open-ended designs adapt to more inputs; constrained contracts are easier to test and operate.
  • Automation vs oversight: More automation reduces manual work but needs stronger logging, review points, and rollback paths.
