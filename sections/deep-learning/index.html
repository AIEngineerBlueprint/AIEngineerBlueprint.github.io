Learning track

Deep Learning

A production-focused path for mastering Deep Learning through concepts, diagrams, hands-on labs, interview practice, and capstone work.

Track outcomes

By the end of this track, you should be able to reason from first principles, design the architecture, identify failure modes, build a working slice, and explain how the system behaves in production.

In plain words: Deep learning is like an assembly line of pattern detectors. Early stations notice simple patterns; later stations combine them into richer understanding. It explains how modern systems learn from images, audio, text, and complex signals without hand-coding every feature.

Chapter 1 Deep learning overview Deep learning is a subset of machine learning that uses neural networks with many layers to automatically learn complex patterns and representations directly from raw data, without a human manually engineering which features matter. Chapter 2 Tensors A tensor is a container for numbers arranged in one or more dimensions. Chapter 3 Gradient descent Gradient descent is the optimisation algorithm that trains neural networks by repeatedly adjusting weights in the direction that reduces the loss function. Chapter 4 Backpropagation Backpropagation is the algorithm that computes how much each weight in a neural network contributed to the prediction error, so gradient descent knows which direction to adjust each weight. Chapter 5 Activation functions Activation functions introduce non-linearity into neural networks. Chapter 6 Regularization Regularization is a set of techniques that constrain a model during training to reduce overfitting, trading a small amount of training-set accuracy for better generalization to new data. Chapter 7 CNNs Convolutional Neural Networks are an architecture that applies small, shared filters across an input, exploiting the assumption that local spatial patterns matter and the same pattern can appear anywhere in the input, making them well suited to images. Chapter 8 RNNs Recurrent Neural Networks process sequences by maintaining a hidden state updated at each step, carrying information from earlier elements forward, which made them the standard approach for sequential data before transformers became dominant. Chapter 9 Optimization tricks Optimization tricks are practical techniques, learning rate schedules, gradient clipping, momentum-based optimizers, that make training deep networks faster and more stable beyond plain gradient descent. Chapter 10 Hardware basics Hardware basics for deep learning covers the fundamental difference between CPUs and GPUs and why AI training and inference workloads specifically benefit from specialized, highly parallel processors rather than general-purpose ones. Chapter 11 Training loops A training loop is the repeated cycle of feeding a batch of data through the model, computing loss, running backpropagation, and updating weights, repeated across many batches and epochs until the model converges or a stopping criterion is met. Chapter 12 Distributed training Distributed training splits a model's training workload across multiple GPUs or machines, either by dividing the data, data parallelism, or dividing the model itself, model parallelism, to train faster or handle models too large for a single device. Chapter 13 Model serving Model serving is the infrastructure that runs a trained deep learning model against live requests, handling batching, hardware allocation, and latency requirements, distinct from the training process that produced the model. Chapter 14 DL lab The deep learning lab is a hands-on exercise to train a small network with one regularization technique, deliberately compare it against an unregularized version, and observe the difference in the train-validation gap. Chapter 15 Mini project The mini project ships a small, complete deep learning model for one real task, with a documented architecture choice, regularization applied and justified, and a genuine train/validation evaluation. Chapter 16 Capstone The capstone designs a complete deep learning solution for a real problem: architecture choice with justification, a training loop with regularization and optimization tricks, a distributed training plan if needed, and a model-serving plan including latency budget. Chapter 17 Interview pack The deep learning interview pack prepares you to explain regularization, architecture choices like CNNs and RNNs, and training stability tricks with concrete reasoning, backed by the ability to diagnose a described training problem.
