Learning track

AI Governance Strategy

A production-focused path for mastering AI Governance Strategy through concepts, diagrams, hands-on labs, interview practice, and capstone work.

Track outcomes

By the end of this track, you should be able to reason from first principles, design the architecture, identify failure modes, build a working slice, and explain how the system behaves in production.

In plain words: AI governance strategy is an enterprise control tower for AI: policies define safe lanes, shields enforce boundaries, telemetry proves what happened, and dashboards show leaders where risk, cost, and adoption stand. Enterprise AI only scales when governance, security, compliance, identity, data protection, model controls, agent controls, runtime protection, cost management, and audit evidence are designed as one operating system.

Chapter 1 AI governance strategy AI governance strategy is the enterprise operating system for safe AI: policies define what is allowed, shields enforce controls, audit evidence proves what happened, and dashboards show risk, cost, adoption, and compliance. Chapter 2 Governance and policy layer The governance and policy layer defines enterprise rules for AI usage, model approval, data handling, human oversight, audit evidence, and lifecycle governance. Chapter 3 AI security shield layer The AI security shield layer combines controls that protect prompts, retrieval, MCP tools, identity, and AI execution boundaries from abuse or misuse. Chapter 4 Prompt shield Prompt shield protects the instruction layer by detecting injection, jailbreaks, sensitive prompts, unsafe prompt transformations, and attempts to expose system instructions. Chapter 5 RAG shield RAG shield enforces safe retrieval by checking document classification, user access, sensitive data, citation support, poisoning signals, embedding security, and vector database access. Chapter 6 MCP shield MCP shield governs Model Context Protocol servers and tools by verifying server trust, classifying tool risk, enforcing permissions, isolating execution, and logging activity. Chapter 7 Authentication and identity shield Authentication and identity shield verifies users, agents, tools, services, and workloads before they can access AI capabilities or enterprise data. Chapter 8 Data protection shield Data protection shield prevents sensitive data from being exposed, retained, moved, logged, or used in ways that violate policy or regulation. Chapter 9 Model shield Model shield governs which models may be used, how they are scored, routed, evaluated, versioned, rolled back, and detected when unauthorized models appear. Chapter 10 Agent shield Agent shield controls autonomous systems by governing agent identity, authorization, memory, agent-to-agent communication, capability boundaries, action limits, approvals, and behavior monitoring. Chapter 11 API shield API shield protects AI APIs with gateway controls, authentication, authorization, rate limits, payload inspection, threat detection, schema validation, and abuse prevention. Chapter 12 Content safety shield Content safety shield classifies and controls harmful, illegal, brand-risk, or policy-violating content in prompts, outputs, uploaded media, and generated artifacts. Chapter 13 Compliance shield Compliance shield maps AI behavior and evidence to regulations, policies, data residency rules, reporting obligations, and continuous compliance controls. Chapter 14 Supply chain shield Supply chain shield governs AI components such as libraries, models, prompts, packages, datasets, dependencies, and SBOM records. Chapter 15 Runtime protection shield Runtime protection shield detects and enforces policy during live AI execution using anomaly detection, abuse prevention, session monitoring, and real-time policy checks. Chapter 16 Cost and resource governance Cost and resource governance controls AI spend, token usage, quotas, model routing, department budgets, allocation, optimization, and forecasting. Chapter 17 Observability and audit Observability and audit records prompts, responses, retrievals, tools, MCP activity, users, sessions, incidents, and immutable evidence for debugging and governance. Chapter 18 Administration and control plane Administration and control plane is the enterprise UI/API for managing policies, organizations, projects, users, roles, shields, connectors, secrets, models, knowledge sources, MCP servers, integrations, and global settings. Chapter 19 AI operations AIOps AI operations applies production operations to AI systems: health monitoring, latency, accuracy, drift, hallucination detection, eval pipelines, canaries, and incidents. Chapter 20 Analytics and executive dashboard Analytics and executive dashboard turns AI usage, risk, cost, security, compliance, adoption, model behavior, and agent activity into leadership visibility and operating decisions. Chapter 21 Cross-cutting principles Cross-cutting principles are the non-negotiable design rules that apply across every AI governance layer and shield. Chapter 22 Governance lab Governance lab is a hands-on exercise where you design a governed AI workflow with policy-as-code, identity, shields, evidence, cost limits, and dashboard metrics. Chapter 23 Capstone The AI governance strategy capstone designs a complete enterprise governance platform across policy, security shields, data protection, models, agents, APIs, compliance, operations, analytics, and administration. Chapter 24 Interview pack The AI governance strategy interview pack prepares you to explain enterprise AI governance, security shields, policy-as-code, compliance, audit, and executive metrics.
