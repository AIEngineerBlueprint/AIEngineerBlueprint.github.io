Responsible AI · Chapter 8

EU AI Act

The EU AI Act is a risk-based regulatory framework that classifies AI systems into risk tiers, unacceptable, high, limited, and minimal risk, and imposes specific obligations, such as risk assessments, documentation, and human oversight, that scale with the assessed risk tier of the system.

Calculating reading timeHands-on labQuiz

In plain terms

It is like a building code that imposes stricter inspection and safety requirements on a hospital than on a garden shed, because the consequences of failure are far more severe.

How it works

In practice it comes down to classifying an AI system's specific use case into one of the Act's defined risk tiers, then implementing the tier-appropriate obligations, ranging from an outright prohibition for unacceptable-risk uses to risk management, data governance, and human oversight requirements for high-risk systems, down to lighter transparency obligations for limited-risk systems.

A concrete example

An AI system used for employment screening decisions falls into the high-risk tier under the Act, requiring documented risk assessment, human oversight, and technical documentation, while a simple AI-powered spam filter falls into a minimal-risk tier with essentially no specific obligations.

Trade-offs

  • The core tension: Classifying and complying with the appropriate risk tier requires real upfront legal and engineering assessment work, but operating a high-risk system in the EU without meeting its specific obligations creates serious legal exposure once enforcement applies

Hands-on lab

  2. Test it against one normal case and one edge case, and write down both actual outputs.

Done when: the edge case is handled correctly or fails safely, not just the normal case.

Common mistakes

  • The EU AI Act's obligations are not the same for every AI system; the specific requirements depend entirely on which risk tier a system's use case falls into, so assuming a blanket compliance checklist applies to everything misses both over-compliance costs on low-risk systems and under-compliance risk on high-risk ones.
