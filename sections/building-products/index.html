Learning track

Building Products

A production-focused path for mastering Building Products through concepts, diagrams, hands-on labs, interview practice, and capstone work.

Track outcomes

By the end of this track, you should be able to reason from first principles, design the architecture, identify failure modes, build a working slice, and explain how the system behaves in production.

In plain words: Building AI products is like designing a service business with software employees. You define the job, train the workflow, measure quality, and decide when humans must step in. The winning skill is turning model capability into a reliable user outcome with requirements, evaluation, rollout, and operations.

Production build path

Use this track to contribute to one of the platform projects below. These projects are intentionally track-level because they combine multiple chapters into a complete product slice.

Enterprise RAG

Build a private documentation assistant with ingestion, chunking, hybrid search, citations, evals, access control, and freshness monitoring.

AI code reviewer

Build a reviewer that reads diffs, classifies risk, checks security rules, and produces actionable comments with confidence levels.

Document intelligence

Extract structured data from PDFs or forms, validate against schemas, route exceptions to humans, and track extraction quality.

AI support bot

Answer customer questions with grounded knowledge, escalation rules, conversation memory, and deflection/CSAT metrics.

Engineering assistant

Connect repo search, issue context, runbooks, and deployment checks into a tool-using assistant for engineers.

Chapter 1 Product strategy Product strategy decides which user problem an AI capability should solve, why AI is needed, and how success will be measured. Chapter 2 Requirements Requirements translate an AI product idea into explicit capabilities, constraints, data needs, success metrics, and failure behavior. Chapter 3 Architecture Architecture for an AI product is the overall system design connecting the user-facing application, the model or model gateway, retrieval and tool integrations, guardrails, and observability into one coherent, deployable system, distinct from any single component's internal design. Chapter 4 API design API design for AI products defines request/response contracts, streaming behavior, errors, auth, idempotency, and observability fields. Chapter 5 Database design Database design for AI products stores prompts, traces, evals, documents, embeddings, feedback, and user state with clear ownership and retention. Chapter 6 Prompt library A prompt library stores reusable, versioned prompt templates with owners, variables, eval results, and release history. Chapter 7 Testing Testing for AI products combines traditional deterministic software tests for the surrounding application code with AI-specific evaluation for the probabilistic model behavior, since standard unit tests alone cannot verify whether generated output is actually good. Chapter 8 Evaluation Evaluation in the context of building AI products means deciding, at the product level, what quality actually means for this specific feature, accuracy, tone, safety, latency, and building the measurement process that proves whether the product meets that bar before and after every change. Chapter 9 CI/CD CI/CD for AI systems automates tests, evals, builds, deployments, and rollbacks for prompts, models, tools, and application code. Chapter 10 Deployment Deployment moves an AI feature into a controlled runtime with scaling, secrets, networking, monitoring, and rollback in place. Chapter 11 Monitoring Monitoring tracks live AI behavior so teams notice quality, safety, latency, cost, and reliability changes after deployment. Chapter 12 Documentation Documentation for AI products explains behavior, limits, data use, operations, evaluation, and user responsibilities. Chapter 13 Release Release is the coordinated launch of an AI capability with gating, communication, monitoring, feedback collection, and rollback readiness. Chapter 14 Product lab Product lab is the capstone-style practice of turning AI concepts into a runnable product slice with requirements, architecture, cloud deployment variants, smoke tests, evals, observability, security, and release criteria. Chapter 15 Mini project The mini project ships a small, complete AI product feature end to end, including architecture, tests, and evaluation, deployed and documented well enough that another engineer could run, test, and extend it. Chapter 16 Capstone The capstone designs a complete AI product from requirements through architecture, testing and evaluation strategy, deployment, and monitoring, as one coherent plan for a real product idea. Chapter 17 Interview pack The building products interview pack prepares you to discuss end-to-end AI product delivery credibly: translating requirements into evaluation criteria, architecture trade-offs, and testing strategy, backed by a concrete story about a real product you shipped.
