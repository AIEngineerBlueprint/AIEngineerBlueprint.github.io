Ethics baseline

An ethics baseline for AI is the minimum set of commitments an organization makes about fairness, transparency, and harm prevention before deploying an AI system, treated as a non-negotiable starting point rather than an afterthought added if time permits.

Calculating reading timeHands-on labQuiz

In plain terms

It is like a restaurant's baseline food-safety practices: not a competitive differentiator to brag about, just the non-negotiable floor below which the business shouldn't operate at all.

For non-technical readers

The practical takeaway for a non-technical leader: an ethics baseline isn't corporate values language for a webpage, it's a specific checklist, like we tested this doesn't discriminate by group or users can appeal an automated decision, that either gets done before launch or doesn't. The real business risk isn't looking bad in a press release; it's shipping a system that treats certain customers unfairly and discovering that only after it's already caused harm and made the news. Treating the ethics baseline as a launch gate, the same way you'd treat a legal or security sign-off, is far cheaper than the alternative.

How it works

In practice it comes down to defining specific, checkable commitments, such as testing for disparate outcomes across user groups, disclosing when a user is interacting with AI, and having a process for users to contest or escalate an AI decision, and treating these as launch requirements rather than optional enhancements.

A concrete example

A company's ethics baseline requires that any AI system making decisions about people, such as loan approvals or hiring, be tested for outcome disparities across demographic groups before launch, regardless of how much schedule pressure exists.

Trade-offs

  • The core tension: Holding a strict ethics baseline can slow down launches and add real testing overhead, but skipping it to move faster risks harm to real people and significant legal and reputational damage to the business if problems surface after launch

Hands-on lab

Task: For one real or hypothetical AI system your organization might build, write two specific, testable ethics baseline requirements it would need to meet before launch.

  1. Build or sketch the smallest version that actually runs or can be reviewed.
  2. Test it against one normal case and one edge case, and write down both actual outputs.
  3. Note what you would log, and which single metric would reveal this breaking in production.

Done when: the edge case is handled correctly or fails safely, not just the normal case.

Common mistakes

  • Ethics in AI is not a public relations exercise separate from the actual engineering work; a real ethics baseline consists of specific, testable requirements that either get verified before launch or don't, not a values statement that exists independently of what actually ships.
