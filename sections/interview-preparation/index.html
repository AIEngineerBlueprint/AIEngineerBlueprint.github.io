Learning track

Interview Preparation

A production-focused path for mastering Interview Preparation through concepts, diagrams, hands-on labs, interview practice, and capstone work.

Track outcomes

By the end of this track, you should be able to reason from first principles, design the architecture, identify failure modes, build a working slice, and explain how the system behaves in production.

In plain words: Interview preparation is like training for a match: you practice fundamentals, drills, real scenarios, and post-game review. AI engineering interviews test both concepts and judgment: explaining trade-offs matters as much as definitions.

Chapter 1 Interview strategy Interview strategy is the plan for showing the right evidence of skill across behavioral, coding, system design, AI depth, and product judgment rounds. Chapter 2 Behavioral Behavioral interview preparation for AI roles means having concrete, structured stories about your actual decisions, actions, and outcomes, using the STAR structure, situation, task, action, result, ready to answer questions about ownership, conflict, and judgment under pressure. Chapter 3 System design System design interviews evaluate how you turn ambiguous product goals into scalable, reliable, safe architecture with clear trade-offs. Chapter 4 Coding Coding interviews evaluate problem decomposition, correctness, complexity, communication, testing, and debugging under time pressure. Chapter 5 LLM internals LLM internals interviews test whether you understand how tokens, embeddings, attention, training, decoding, and inference constraints shape product behavior. Chapter 6 Agents Agent interview questions test whether you understand agentic system design, task decomposition, tool use, failure handling, and safe autonomy, not just that you know agents can use tools and take multiple steps. Chapter 7 RAG RAG interview questions test your understanding of chunking, retrieval, ranking, and grounding trade-offs, not just that you know retrieval-augmented generation retrieves documents before generating an answer. Chapter 8 Security Security interview questions for AI roles test whether you can identify and defend against LLM-specific threats, prompt injection, data leakage, excessive tool permissions, not just general software security knowledge. Chapter 9 Architecture Architecture interview questions test your ability to design a complete AI system, connecting the application, model or model gateway, retrieval, guardrails, and observability into one coherent design, not just describing which model API to call. Chapter 10 Leadership Leadership interviews evaluate ownership, influence, conflict handling, judgment, and ability to improve teams and systems. Chapter 11 Cloud Cloud interview questions for AI roles test your ability to justify specific service choices, compute, storage, identity, for a real deployment, not just name AWS or Azure services you're aware of. Chapter 12 Prompt engineering Prompt engineering interview questions test your ability to design, test, and version prompts reliably in production, not just that you know techniques like few-shot examples or chain-of-thought. Chapter 13 Mock interviews Mock interviews simulate real interview pressure so you can measure and improve structure, correctness, timing, and recovery from follow-ups. Chapter 14 Interview lab The interview lab is a hands-on exercise to answer one real technical interview question out loud under a time limit, record it, and score your own answer against a rubric before rewriting the weakest part. Chapter 15 Question bank A Question bank A is a curated first set of realistic AI engineering interview questions spanning system design, coding, and applied reasoning, meant to be practiced with timed, structured answers rather than passively read. Chapter 16 Question bank B Question bank B is a second curated set of realistic AI engineering interview questions covering behavioral, architecture, and safety-and-security topics, complementing question bank A's more technical focus, meant for the same active, timed practice. Chapter 17 Evaluation rubric An evaluation rubric defines the criteria used to score an interview answer, AI output, or engineering artifact consistently.
